SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS



Goal 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth

  • General Description

    • Goal 8 of the SDGs, "Decent Work and Economic Growth," aims to promote sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment, and decent work for all. This goal recognizes the importance of creating opportunities for meaningful and productive employment, fostering entrepreneurship, and ensuring that economic growth benefits everyone, especially those in vulnerable situations.

  • Targets

    • Full Employment: Achieve full and productive employment, including for women and young people, and equal pay for work of equal value.

    • Decent Work: Promote safe and secure working environments, eradicate forced labor, modern slavery, and child labor, and ensure decent working conditions for all.

    • Youth Employment: Implement policies that promote sustainable tourism, create jobs, and promote local culture and products.

    • Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs): Encourage the growth of small and medium-sized enterprises, which are often important drivers of employment and economic growth.

  • Progress


    • Efforts have been made to promote decent work and economic growth, leading to increased employment opportunities and reduced poverty rates in some regions. The focus on promoting entrepreneurship and innovation has contributed to economic diversification and job creation.However, challenges persist in addressing informal employment, unemployment rates, and disparities in job opportunities. The COVID-19 pandemic also highlighted vulnerabilities in labor markets and underscored the importance of social protection and job stability.

    • Achieving Goal 8 requires policies and strategies that prioritize job creation, labor rights, and equal opportunities for all. Efforts to improve working conditions, eliminate exploitative practices, and promote skills development are essential to fostering economic growth that benefits individuals and communities.
