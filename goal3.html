SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS



Goal 3: Good Health and Well-being

  • General Description

    • Goal 3 of the SDGs, "Good Health and Well-being," aims to ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for people of all ages. This goal recognizes that good health is not just the absence of diseases but also includes mental and social well-being. Achieving this goal requires addressing a wide range of health issues, improving access to healthcare, and promoting healthy lifestyles.

  • Targets

    • Maternal and Child Health : Reduce maternal mortality and end preventable deaths of newborns and children under five years of age. Ensure universal access to sexual and reproductive health services.

    • Infectious Diseases : End the epidemics of AIDS, tuberculosis, malaria, and neglected tropical diseases. Strengthen the prevention and treatment of substance abuse, including drug and alcohol abuse.

    • Non-Communicable Diseases : Reduce the number of premature deaths from non-communicable diseases through prevention, treatment, and promotion of mental health and well-being.

    • Universal Health Coverage: Achieve universal health coverage, including financial risk protection, access to quality essential healthcare services, and access to safe, effective, quality, and affordable essential medicines and vaccines.

  • Progress


    • Progress has been made in improving various health indicators globally, including reductions in child mortality, improved access to clean water and sanitation, and increased coverage of essential vaccinations. Efforts to combat infectious diseases like HIV/AIDS, tuberculosis, and malaria have also yielded positive results.However, challenges remain in achieving universal health coverage, addressing the burden of non-communicable diseases, and ensuring access to quality healthcare for vulnerable populations. The COVID-19 pandemic further underscored the importance of strong health systems, preparedness, and the need to prioritize health for all.

    • Achieving Goal 3 requires a comprehensive approach that includes improving healthcare infrastructure, promoting health education and awareness, addressing environmental factors affecting health, and ensuring equitable access to healthcare services and treatments.
